Housing

Properly designed housing protects chickens from predators, weather extremes, and promotes good ventilation. Provide adequate space to prevent overcrowding, which can lead to stress and health issues.

Water Considerations

Ensure that water is free from contaminants, pathogens, and debris. Regularly clean water containers and troughs.
Monitor pH levels; ideally, water should be between 6.5 and 8.5. High acidity or alkalinity can affect chicken health.

Nutrition

Offer a complete feed that meets the nutritional requirements for growth, egg production, and overall health.
Ensure constant access to clean, fresh water.

Temperature and Ventilation

Maintain optimal temperature and humidity levels to prevent heat stress or respiratory problems.
Ensure good airflow to reduce moisture and ammonia buildup.
